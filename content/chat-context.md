# Research and AI

How I research before I build, and how AI makes it faster. Justin keeps a steady stream of product signals coming in and runs a clear research loop for each project. He uses Claude and Cursor at each step to sort information, highlight what matters, and move faster without skipping judgment.

**Always-on signal sources**: Front App (support tickets, CSM interactions) and PostHog (feature usage events, user identification). Slack sends these events into one place so important signals are easy to see.

**Research loop (per initiative)**: (1) Review existing feedback and summarize patterns with Claude. (2) Talk with internal teammates; Claude helps prepare discussion guides and role-play. (3) Find customers to talk to; Claude writes outreach based on usage. (4) Customer interviews; Claude summarizes notes and key quotes. (5) First design ideas (three approaches first); Cursor and Figma MCP for prototypes. (6) Internal review; Claude stress-tests with likely stakeholder concerns. (7) Customer review; Claude summarizes feedback, Cursor makes changes between sessions.
